Bruschetta Recipe

Bruschetta with a classic balsamic glaze

Bruschetta is a classic Italian appetizer that consists of grilled or toasted bread that is rubbed with garlic and drizzled with olive oil. It is typically topped with fresh tomato, basil, and mozzarella cheese, and sometimes with other ingredients such as balsamic vinegar, parmesan cheese, or prosciutto. The combination of the crunchy bread, juicy tomatoes, fresh basil, and melted cheese creates a flavorful and satisfying bite. Bruschetta is easy to make and is perfect for serving at a dinner party, as a snack, or as a light meal.

Ingrediants

  • 4 medium ripe tomatoes
  • 1/4 fresh chopped basil leaves
  • 4 cloves of garlic
  • salt + pepper to taste
  • 1/4C olive oil
  • 1 baguette
  • 4 oz fresh mozzarella
  • balsamic glaze

Directions

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C)
  2. Chop the tomatoes into small pieces and place in a medium bowl
  3. Add the chopped basil, minced garlic, olive oil, salt, and pepper to the bowl and mix well
  4. Cut the baguette into 1/2-inch thick slices
  5. Arrange the slices on a baking sheet and brush both sides with olive oil
  6. Bake for 10-12 minutes, or until lightly toasted
  7. Remove from the oven and top each slice with a slice of mozzarella cheese
  8. Place the baking sheet back in the oven and bake for another 5 minutes, or until the cheese has melted
  9. Spoon the tomato mixture onto the toasted baguette slices
  10. Drizzle with balsamic glaze, if desired
